# Single-Cell Foundation Models

## Summary
Single-cell foundation models, exemplified by scGPT, represent a paradigm shift in computational biology by creating universal pre-trained models capable of addressing diverse single-cell analysis tasks. Built on transformer architectures, these foundation models are trained on massive collections of single-cell multi-omics data, learning generalizable representations of gene expression patterns, cellular states, and biological functions. scGPT specifically demonstrates the power of self-supervised pre-training on large-scale transcriptomic datasets, enabling the model to perform cell type annotation, perturbation response prediction, gene regulatory inference, and multi-omics integration without task-specific training. The foundation model approach mirrors successful strategies in natural language processing and computer vision, where large pre-trained models achieve superior performance through transfer learning. These models capture intricate biological relationships—gene-gene interactions, cell-cell communication, and developmental trajectories—that emerge from exposure to diverse experimental conditions and tissue types. The generative capabilities of these models extend beyond classification tasks, enabling in silico perturbation experiments and counterfactual predictions of cellular responses to genetic or chemical interventions. As single-cell sequencing technologies generate increasingly large and complex datasets, foundation models provide a scalable framework for extracting biological insights, democratizing advanced computational analysis, and accelerating discoveries in developmental biology, disease mechanisms, and therapeutic target identification.

## Key Themes
- Pre-trained transformer models for single-cell analysis
- Multi-task learning across diverse biological applications
- Generative modeling of cellular states and perturbation responses
- Scalable frameworks for multi-omics integration
