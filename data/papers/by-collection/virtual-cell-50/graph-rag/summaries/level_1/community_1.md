# Computational Physiology and Multi-scale Biological Modeling

## Summary
This community centers on developing computational frameworks and standardized formats for modeling physiological systems across multiple spatial and temporal scales. Chaste (Cancer, Heart and Soft Tissue Environment) emerges as a comprehensive computational infrastructure for simulating biological tissues, with particular strength in cardiac electrophysiology modeling. The framework supports discrete cell-based models and continuous partial differential equations, enabling researchers to bridge molecular, cellular, tissue, and organ-level phenomena. CellML, an XML-based markup language, provides the standardization layer necessary for encoding and exchanging mathematical models of cellular and physiological processes. This standardization facilitates model reuse, verification, and integration across research groups. The Physiome Project represents the broader vision of creating quantitative, integrative models of human physiology that span from genes to whole organisms. These tools collectively address the challenge of representing complex biological systems where behavior at one scale emerges from interactions at lower scales. Cardiac electrophysiology serves as a key application domain where computational models can predict arrhythmias, drug effects, and disease progression. The community emphasizes open standards, reproducible modeling practices, and software infrastructure that supports long-term maintenance and collaborative development of physiological models.

## Key Themes
- Multi-scale modeling spanning molecular to organ-level physiology
- Standardized model representation and exchange formats (CellML)
- Software infrastructure for tissue and cardiac electrophysiology simulation
