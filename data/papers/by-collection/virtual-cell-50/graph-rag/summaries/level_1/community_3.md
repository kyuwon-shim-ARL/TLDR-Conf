# Large Language Models for Biological Language

## Summary
The intersection of large language models (LLMs) and single-cell biology represents a transformative approach to biological data interpretation. Cell2Sentence and related frameworks establish a novel paradigm by translating high-dimensional single-cell transcriptomic profiles into natural language representations, enabling cross-modal learning between biological and linguistic domains. These methods leverage transformer architectures—originally designed for natural language processing—to capture complex cellular states, gene expression patterns, and biological functions in interpretable text formats. By converting cell-to-text representations, researchers can apply the vast capabilities of pre-trained language models to biological questions, facilitating tasks such as cell type identification, functional annotation, and cross-dataset integration. The interpretability advantage is particularly significant: where traditional embedding spaces remain opaque, text-based representations provide human-readable descriptions of cellular states, making model predictions more transparent and scientifically actionable. This approach also enables zero-shot transfer learning, where knowledge from one biological context can be applied to novel cell types or experimental conditions without retraining. The integration of biological language with natural language processing opens new possibilities for automated literature mining, hypothesis generation, and knowledge discovery across the rapidly expanding landscape of single-cell genomics data.

## Key Themes
- Cross-modal learning between biological data and natural language
- Interpretability and explainability of cellular representations
- Transformer architectures for biological sequence analysis
- Zero-shot transfer learning in single-cell biology
