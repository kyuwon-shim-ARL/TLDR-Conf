# Systems Biology Simulation and Biochemical Pathway Modeling

## Summary
This community focuses on computational platforms for simulating biochemical pathways and physiological systems, with COPASI (Complex Pathway Simulator) serving as a flagship tool for deterministic and stochastic modeling of biochemical networks. COPASI provides researchers with a comprehensive environment for constructing kinetic models, performing parameter estimation, and analyzing system dynamics through steady-state analysis, time-course simulations, and sensitivity analysis. The platform supports multiple simulation algorithms, allowing researchers to choose between deterministic ordinary differential equations for large molecule numbers or stochastic methods for systems where molecular fluctuations matter. Parameter estimation capabilities enable researchers to fit models to experimental data using various optimization algorithms, addressing the challenge of determining rate constants and binding affinities from incomplete measurements. Sensitivity analysis tools help identify which parameters most strongly influence model behavior, guiding experimental design and model refinement. Beyond general biochemical pathways, the community extends to specialized physiological models such as cardiac electrophysiology, where detailed mathematical descriptions capture cardiac action potential generation and propagation. These cardiac models integrate ion channel kinetics, calcium handling, and metabolic coupling to simulate normal heart function and disease states like arrhythmias. The community emphasizes quantitative systems biology approaches where mathematical models complement experimental work, enabling mechanistic hypothesis testing and predictive simulations that guide therapeutic interventions.

- Parameter estimation and sensitivity analysis for model calibration
- Specialized physiological modeling including cardiac electrophysiology
