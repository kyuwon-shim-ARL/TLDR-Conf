# Network Pharmacology and Metabolomics Integration

## Summary
This community focuses on systems-level approaches to drug discovery and precision medicine through network pharmacology and metabolomics. Network pharmacology represents a paradigm shift from the traditional "one drug, one target" model to a holistic understanding of how drugs interact with multiple targets within complex biological networks. This approach maps drug-target networks to identify polypharmacological effects, synergistic interactions, and potential side effects that emerge from systems-level perturbations. Artificial intelligence enhances precision medicine by integrating multi-omics data with clinical outcomes to predict personalized treatment responses. The Human Metabolome Database (HMDB) serves as a critical knowledge resource, cataloging thousands of metabolites with their chemical structures, biological roles, and disease associations. Systems pharmacology extends network approaches by incorporating pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic modeling to predict how drugs behave in living systems. Metabolomics provides the experimental foundation by measuring small molecule profiles that reflect cellular metabolism, disease states, and drug responses. The integration of metabolomics data with drug-target networks enables researchers to identify biomarkers for drug efficacy, understand mechanism of action, and discover new therapeutic opportunities. This community emphasizes computational tools that can handle the complexity of multi-target drugs and the heterogeneity of patient populations in the era of personalized medicine.

- Metabolomics databases and systems pharmacology frameworks
