### 1.4 Research Fields

Dr. Song is a **computational biologist and systems engineer** whose research bridges chemical engineering, microbial ecology, and bioinformatics. His core expertise areas include:

- **Genome-scale metabolic network modeling** (flux balance analysis, constraint-based methods)
- **Cybernetic modeling** of cellular metabolism (dynamic metabolic regulation)
- **Microbial community modeling** (interspecies interactions, community assembly)
- **Data-driven and machine learning approaches** for biological systems
- **Ecological network analysis** and multi-omics data integration
- **Reactive-transport modeling** coupled with microbial metabolism
- **Microbiome engineering** (gut microbiome, soil microbiome, synthetic consortia)

His overarching research vision is to develop computational and modeling tools that can **predict microbial community behavior and metabolic function**, enabling rational design of microbial systems for biotechnology, human health, and environmental applications.

## 7. Lab Assessment: Song Research Group

### 7.1 Research Programs and Themes

The Song Research Group (self-styled "Habongers") focuses on **developing innovative modeling and computational tools to address key challenges in biological, life, and environmental sciences**. Their methodology integrates:

1. **Process-based (mechanistic) modeling**: Genome-scale metabolic models, flux balance analysis, cybernetic models, agent-based models
2. **Data-driven modeling**: Machine learning, network inference, multi-omics integration
3. **Ecological theory**: Interspecies interaction prediction, community assembly rules

#### Active Research Areas

| Research Theme | Description | Key Applications |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Metabolic Network Modeling** | Genome-scale metabolic reconstruction and constraint-based analysis | Predicting metabolic capabilities of individual organisms |
| **Microbial Community Modeling** | Multi-species metabolic models, interspecies interaction prediction (MIIA framework) | Soil microbiome, gut microbiome, synthetic consortia |
| **Cybernetic Modeling** | Dynamic metabolic regulation modeling based on optimal resource allocation | Bioreactor design, multi-substrate utilization |
| **Reactive-Transport + Metabolism** | Coupling FBA with reactive transport models using ML surrogates | Subsurface biogeochemistry, environmental remediation |
| **Gut Microbiome Modeling** | Diet-microbiome-health interactions, glycan-mediated community dynamics | Nebraska Food for Health Center initiatives |
| **Subsurface Hydrogen Modeling** | Microbial interactions with geological hydrogen | NSF RAISE clean energy project |
| **CO2 Bioconversion** | Metabolic modeling for acetate-based microbial synthesis | NSF ECO-CBET bioelectrochemical manufacturing |

### 7.2 Notable Software and Computational Tools

- **MIIA (Minimal Interspecies Interaction Adjustment)**: A computational framework for predicting context-dependent (neighbor-dependent) microbial interactions. Published in *Frontiers in Microbiology* (2019) and highlighted by DOE BER.
- **FBA-ML coupling**: Machine learning surrogates for flux balance analysis to accelerate reactive-transport simulations by orders of magnitude. Published in *Scientific Reports* (2025).
- **GitHub repositories**: 10 repositories available at [github.com/hyunseobsong](https://github.com/hyunseobsong).
