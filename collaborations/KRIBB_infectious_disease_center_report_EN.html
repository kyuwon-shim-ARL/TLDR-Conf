Research Background

4-1

Dr. Ryu: "From the Scent of Soil Bacteria to Infection Prevention"

A 20-year research journey told in five chapters. Click each chapter to expand the full story.

1
2003
The Scent of Bacteria Grows Plants ▼

Dr. Ryu asked a question of soil bacteria: "How do you communicate with plants?" The answer was scent. He was the first in the world to discover that an invisible chemical (2,3-butanediol) released by Bacillus bacteria can boost plant growth up to 5-fold. Published in PNAS, 1,605 citations.

Volatile organic compounds (VOCs) released by bacteria float through the air and are absorbed through the stomata in plant leaves. This "chemical letter" activates the growth hormone pathways in plant cells, causing larger roots and more leaves.
Simple Analogy
Just as the smell of a neighboring bakery whets your appetite, bacteria's "scent letter" alone makes plants grow more vigorously — growth is promoted through airborne chemical signals, without any direct contact.
2
2004
The Same Scent Also Awakens Plant Immunity ▼

Remarkably, the same scent molecules also pre-activate the plant's immune system. This is called "Induced Systemic Resistance (ISR)" — like having guards already stationed before the pathogen arrives. Published in Phytopathology, 1,628 citations.

ISR involves plants receiving a "threat signal" in advance and switching immune-related genes into a primed state. When a real pathogen invades, they respond 2–3 times faster and more powerfully than unprepared plants.
Simple Analogy — Fire Drill
Fire drill = beneficial bacteria send "be prepared" signal  →  Standby state = plant knows escape routes in advance  →  Real fire = when pathogen arrives, responds much faster. The key is not killing the pathogen directly, but "preparing to fight in advance."
3
2008
A "Health Management Team" That Works Against Drought and Salt Too ▼

This principle extended beyond disease defense to also work against environmental stresses like drought and salinity. Proof that bacteria serve as a plant's "health management team." Published in Trends in Plant Science, 1,813 citations — his most-cited paper.

Rhizosphere bacteria provide plants with three services:
  • Stress counselor: reduces stress hormones (ethylene) to calm the plant
  • Nutritionist: converts phosphate and iron in soil into forms the plant can absorb
  • Fitness trainer: promotes wider root development to improve water and nutrient uptake
Dr. Ryu's key insight at this point: "Microbial chemical signals are a master key capable of awakening the host's entire defense system."
4
2020~
COVID-19 and Superbacteria — From Plants to Humans ▼

A major pivot during the pandemic: applying the principles of plant immunity to human infectious disease. Developed Korea's first-ever COVID-19 primate infection model, and selectively eliminated superbacteria using gold nanoparticle "Trojan horses."

COVID-19 Response: Built a COVID-19 infection model using macaques. 4th worldwide, first in Korea. This model allowed Korean companies to validate the efficacy of therapeutics and vaccines.

Superbacteria Control: Developed a "Trojan horse" strategy by attaching antibiotics to gold nanoparticles to selectively destroy only the superbacteria's cell membrane (ACS Nano, 2025). Normal cells remain unaffected.
5
2026
DDM Immune Priming — The Pinnacle of 20 Years of Research ▼

Proving that the principle of "pre-awakening immunity" discovered in plants also works in mammals. A single dose of DDM simultaneously blocks three superbacteria and influenza virus. A curiosity that began with a handful of soil has led to a solution for humanity's most urgent threats.

In 2003, there was a simple discovery: "bacterial scent → plant growth promotion." The principle hidden within that discovery — a single chemical signal can awaken the host's entire defense system — came to fruition as DDM immune priming after 20 years. A remarkable example of how far a researcher's curiosity can travel.
Journey Summary
Soil bacteria VOC (2003) → Plant immune priming ISR (2004) → Environmental stress tolerance (2008) → COVID-19 model / Nano-antibiotics (2020~) → DDM broad-spectrum infection prevention (2026)

Dr. Seo: "Witnessing Vaccine Failure Led Him to Find a Way to Strengthen Immunity Itself"

A story of three career stages converging toward a single research goal. The lesson from each stage becomes the seed for the next.

1
2011 — 2020 · Seoul National University, College of Veterinary Medicine
Swine Virus Specialist
PhD in Veterinary Virology + Visiting Research at University of Nebraska-Lincoln (2012)
Studied viruses infecting pigs (PCV2 circovirus, PRRSV reproductive and respiratory syndrome virus). These diseases cause enormous damage to the global swine industry. He compared the efficacy of four commercial vaccines and was the first in Korea to discover a mutant virus that existing vaccines could not counter (2014). He also developed expertise as an "animal infection model" specialist, directly infecting animals with viruses and precisely measuring immune responses.
Key Lesson from This Stage

"Vaccines targeting specific pathogens have inherent limitations" — when a virus mutates, vaccines are neutralized. The same principle explains why booster shots are needed for COVID variants.

2
2017 — 2020 · Korea Ginseng Corporation (KGC)
Where Ginseng Meets Immunity
Senior Researcher, KGC Phytomedicine Laboratory
Conducted scientific validation of red ginseng product safety. A 12-month long-term safety study of red ginseng confirmed improved immune function and reduced viral infections. He bridged academia and industry, developing a keen sense for regulatory requirements and practical application.
Key Lesson from This Stage

"When a safe natural substance strengthens immunity, infections decrease" — this observation became the seed of the later DDM research. The origin of the idea: use an already-validated safe substance to enhance immunity.

3
2020 — Present · KRIBB Infectious Disease Research Center
A New Paradigm for Infection Prevention
Principal Researcher — Corresponding Author, DDM Immune Priming
All experiences converge. Animal infection model design capability built at SNU, the insight of "boost immunity with a safe substance" gained at KGC, and the conviction from vaccine failure experience that "we must strengthen immunity itself, not target the pathogen" — all come together. Result: As corresponding author (principal investigator) of the DDM immune priming research, he developed a strategy achieving 100% survival against three types of lethal superbacteria.
Integration of Three Stages

Animal model expertise (SNU) + immune enhancement with safe substance (KGC) + resolve to overcome vaccine limitations = DDM broad-spectrum infection prevention strategy

Key Terminology — Jargon Made Simple

Click each card to expand the explanation.

Induced Systemic Resistance (ISR)
Immunology · Plant Pathology
Similar in principle to giving plants a pre-emptive "vaccine." Beneficial bacteria pre-train the plant's immune system so that when the actual pathogen arrives later, the plant responds faster and more powerfully. ISR = Induced Systemic Resistance.
Bacterial Volatile Compounds (VOC)
Microbiology · Chemical Ecology
"Scent substances" emitted by bacteria. They send chemical signals to plants through the air. 2,3-butanediol is a representative example; they influence plant growth and immunity without direct contact.
Innate Immunity
Immunology
Immunity present from birth. A "security system" that immediately detects any intruder, regardless of pathogen type, and responds rapidly. This is precisely the immunity that DDM enhances.
Adaptive Immunity
Immunology
Immunity that remembers specific pathogens and mounts a targeted response. This is the principle vaccines exploit. The first response is slow, but from the second exposure onward it is fast and strong. Limitation: effectiveness may wane against mutated viruses.
Immune Priming
Immunology · Novel Concept
Putting immune cells into a "combat-ready" state in advance. Establishes a defensive posture before any pathogen arrives. Unlike vaccines, it does not memorize a specific pathogen — instead it rapidly switches on the immune response itself.
Neutrophil
Immunology · Cell Biology
Immune cells comprising 60–70% of white blood cells. The "special forces" that rush to the scene first when bacteria invade. DDM pre-activates these cells, putting them in a combat-ready state.
Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)
Infectious Disease · Public Health
The phenomenon in which bacteria adapt to antibiotics and are no longer killed by them. The root cause of the "superbacteria" problem. The WHO lists AMR as one of the most serious health threats humanity faces.
MRSA / CRPA / CREC
Superbacteria
Representative superbacteria. MRSA = Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus, CRPA = Carbapenem-resistant Pseudomonas aeruginosa, CREC = Carbapenem-resistant Escherichia coli. Existing antibiotics are largely ineffective, resulting in high mortality rates in hospital-acquired infections.
h-index
Researcher Impact Metric
A measure of researcher impact: "h papers have each been cited at least h times." The higher the value, the more consistently influential the researcher's work. Dr. Ryu's h=71 is at the global top 1% level.
Corresponding Author
Author Role in Papers
The "principal investigator" of a paper. The key leader who planned the research and conducted the final review. Dr. Seo's role as corresponding author of the DDM paper means he holds core responsibility for the study's design and findings.
Circovirus (PCV2)
Veterinary Virology
A small circular virus that destroys pig immunity. One of the greatest threats to the global swine industry, causing enormous economic damage worldwide. This was Dr. Seo's early research subject.
Vaccine Failure
Infectious Disease · Immunology
The phenomenon of getting sick despite being vaccinated. Viral mutation is the primary cause. This experience guided Dr. Seo toward the research direction of "strengthening immunity itself, not targeting specific pathogens."
