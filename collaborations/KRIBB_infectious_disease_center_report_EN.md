## 4. Research Background: Understanding Their Work

> The following explains both researchers' work in plain language, accessible to anyone encountering it for the first time — no jargon required.

### 4-1. Dr. Choong-Min Ryu: "From the Smell of Soil Bacteria to Infectious Disease Prevention"

#### The Research Journey as a Story

**Chapter 1 — A Bacterium's Scent Grows Plants (2003)**

Dr. Ryu posed a question to the bacteria living in soil: "How do you communicate with plants?" The answer was **smell**. He was the first in the world to discover that an invisible chemical (2,3-butanediol) emitted by *Bacillus* bacteria can boost plant growth by up to five-fold.

Simple analogy: Just as the aroma drifting from a nearby bakery triggers your appetite, a bacterium's "scent letter" alone is enough to make a plant grow more vigorously.

**Chapter 2 — The Same Scent Wakes Up a Plant's Immune System (2004)**

Remarkably, the same chemical signal also **pre-activates the plant's immune system**. This is called "Induced Systemic Resistance (ISR)."

Simple analogy: Think of it as a school fire drill.
- Fire drill = Beneficial bacteria send a "be prepared" signal
- Standby mode = The plant pre-maps its emergency evacuation routes
- Actual fire = When a pathogen eventually arrives, the plant responds far more quickly

Key insight: Rather than killing the pathogen directly, the strategy is to **"have the fight won before it begins."**

**Chapter 3 — It Works Against Drought and Salt Too (2008)**

This principle extended beyond pathogen defense to environmental stresses like drought and salinity. The bacteria act as a "plant health-management team":
- Stress counselor: Reduces stress hormones to calm the plant
- Nutritionist: Converts soil nutrients into forms the plant can absorb
- Fitness trainer: Promotes wider root development to enhance water and nutrient uptake

The core insight crystallized here: **"Microbial chemical signals can serve as a master key that awakens the host's entire defense system."**

**Chapter 4 — COVID-19 and Super Bacteria (2020–)**

During the pandemic, Dr. Ryu began a major pivot — applying the principles of plant immunity to human infectious disease:
- Developed Korea's first non-human primate infection model essential for testing COVID-19 vaccines and therapeutics
- Used gold nanoparticles in a "Trojan horse" strategy to selectively eliminate only drug-resistant bacteria (ACS Nano, 2025)

**Chapter 5 — DDM Immune Priming (2026): The Culmination of 20 Years of Research**

He demonstrates that the "pre-awakening of immunity" principle discovered in plants works in mammals as well. A single safe compound — DDM — simultaneously blocks three species of super bacteria and influenza. **A curiosity that began with a handful of soil has led to a solution for some of humanity's most urgent threats.**

### 4-2. Dr. Hwi Won Seo: "A Witness to Vaccine Failure Who Chose to Strengthen Immunity Itself"

#### The Career as a Story

**Stage 1: Expert in Pig Viruses (Seoul National University)**

At SNU's College of Veterinary Medicine, Dr. Seo studied viruses that infect pigs — PCV2 and PRRSV.
- PCV2 (Porcine Circovirus Type 2): A tiny virus that destroys pig immunity, causing massive losses across the global swine industry
- He compared the efficacy of four commercially available vaccines and **was the first in Korea to discover a mutant strain of the virus against which existing vaccines were ineffective** (2014)

Core lesson from this period: **"Vaccines targeting a specific pathogen have inherent limitations"** — when a virus mutates, vaccines become powerless (the same principle that necessitates COVID-19 booster shots against new variants).

During this time, he also became an expert in **designing animal infection models** — directly inoculating animals with viruses and precisely measuring immune responses.

**Stage 2: Where Ginseng Meets Immunity (Korea Ginseng Corporation)**

Dr. Seo conducted research to scientifically validate the safety of red ginseng products.
- A 12-month long-term safety study of red ginseng intake confirmed improvements in immune function and reductions in viral infection
- He observed that **"a safe, natural substance that boosts immunity leads to fewer infections"** — an observation that would later become the seed of the DDM research

**Stage 3: A New Paradigm for Infectious Disease Prevention (KRIBB, 2020–present)**

All prior experience converges into a single stream:
- **Animal infection model design expertise** built at SNU
- **The insight that "safe substances can enhance immunity"** gained at KGC
- **The conviction that "we must strengthen immunity itself, not just target individual pathogens"** born from witnessing vaccine failure

Result: As **corresponding author (principal investigator)** of the DDM immune priming study, Dr. Seo develops a strategy achieving **100% survival rates** against three species of lethal super bacteria.

### 4-3. DDM Immune Priming: Where Their Research Converges

#### What Is DDM?

DDM (n-dodecyl-β-D-maltoside) is a **soap-like molecule** — a sugar molecule with a fatty chain attached. It is an FDA-approved compound that has been used for decades as a pharmaceutical excipient (stabilizer), with its human safety profile already well established.

#### How Is This Different from Existing Approaches?

| Approach | Antibiotics | Vaccines | DDM Immune Priming |
|----------|-------------|----------|--------------------|
| **Principle** | Kills bacteria directly | Generates antibodies targeting a specific pathogen | **Enhances the body's own immune capacity** |
| **Resistance problem** | Super bacteria emerge | Mutant viruses escape | **Not applicable** (immune cells do not develop "resistance") |
| **Scope** | Specific bacteria only | Specific pathogen only | **Bacteria + viruses simultaneously** |

#### How Does It Work?

Using a military analogy:
- **Without DDM**: Guards (neutrophils) are resting. They respond slowly when an intruder (pathogen) arrives
- **After DDM administration**: Guards are on patrol wearing bulletproof vests. Intruders are neutralized the moment they appear
- **If no intruder comes**: No harm to residents (normal cells). No overreaction (chronic inflammation)

#### How Striking Are the Experimental Results?

| Infection Condition | DDM-treated Group | Control Group |
|--------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Lethal MRSA (Methicillin-resistant *Staphylococcus aureus*) | **100% survival** | All died |
| Lethal CRPA (Carbapenem-resistant *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*) | **100% survival** | All died |
| Lethal CREC (Carbapenem-resistant *Escherichia coli*) | **100% survival** | All died |
| Influenza | Viral load reduced | High mortality |

#### How Far Away Is Real-World Application?

The research is currently at the mouse experiment stage. Because DDM is already an FDA-approved compound, some safety steps may be expedited, but human clinical trials will be required — making the timeline approximately 5–10 years.

### 4-4. Glossary of Key Terms

| Technical Term | Plain-Language Explanation |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Induced Systemic Resistance (ISR)** | Similar in principle to giving a plant a "vaccine" in advance. Beneficial bacteria train the plant's immune system before any threat arrives |
| **Bacterial volatile compounds (VOCs)** | "Scent molecules" emitted by bacteria that send chemical signals to plants through the air |
| **Innate immunity** | Immunity present from birth. A "guard system" that detects any invader immediately, regardless of type |
| **Adaptive immunity** | Immunity that remembers specific pathogens and mounts a tailored response. The principle exploited by vaccines |
| **Immune priming** | Putting immune cells into a "combat-ready" state in advance |
| **Neutrophils** | Immune cells comprising 60–70% of white blood cells. The "special forces unit" that rushes to the site of bacterial invasion first |
| **Antimicrobial resistance (AMR)** | The phenomenon in which bacteria adapt to antibiotics so that drugs no longer work — the underlying cause of the "super bacteria" crisis |
| **MRSA/CRPA/CREC** | Representative super bacteria strains against which existing antibiotics are largely ineffective |
| **h-index** | A measure of a researcher's impact. "h papers have each been cited at least h times." A higher value indicates consistently influential research over time |
| **Corresponding author** | The "principal investigator" of a paper — the person who conceived the research and is responsible for final review and approval |
| **Porcine circovirus (PCV2)** | A small circular virus that destroys pig immunity. One of the greatest threats to the global swine industry |
| **Vaccine failure** | When a vaccinated individual still contracts the disease. Viral mutation is the primary cause |
